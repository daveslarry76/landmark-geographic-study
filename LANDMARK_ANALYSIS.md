## Historical Background

- **Empire State Building:** The Empire State Building is a 102‑story Art Deco skyscraper in Midtown Manhattan, New York City. Construction began on March 17 1930 and was completed in May 1931 during the Great Depression. Designed by Shreve, Lamb & Harmon, it stood as the world’s tallest building (1,454 ft/443 m) until 1970. It remains a cultural icon and major tourist attraction.
- **Willis Tower:** Originally the Sears Tower, the Willis Tower is a 110‑story skyscraper in Chicago. Designed by Bruce Graham and Fazlur Khan of Skidmore, Owings & Merrill, construction ran from 1970‑1973. At 1,450 ft (442 m) to the roof and 1,729 ft (527 m) with its antenna, it was the world’s tallest building from 1973‑1998. Renamed Willis Tower in 2009 after the Willis Group became a major tenant.
- **Space Needle:** The Space Needle is a 605‑ft (184 m) observation tower in Seattle, completed for the 1962 World’s Fair. Designed by Edward Carlson and John Graham, its distinctive “flying saucer” shape has become a symbol of Seattle and the Pacific Northwest.
